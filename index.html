The Life of Pallan Katgara

Dedicated Businessman and Family Man

Early Life

Pallan Adi Katgara was born on April 22, 1959 in Mumbai, India. His mom is Jeroo and his dad was named Adi. He was the youngest of four children. He has one brother, Homi, and two sisters, Mehre and Hilla. In school he would become the captain of the swim team and co-captain of the tennis team. His father owned many businesses such as one of the most popular freight shipping businesses in India clled Jeena. It was started by his grandfather and has stayed in the Katgara family since then.

Pallan worked in the travel business as well as working for the family business offshoot called Watermaker India. This company hosts the technology to create drinking water from the humidity in the air. Governments would buy the product for villages in India. This saves lives and helps stop the women from having to carry the water in large pots on their heads.

World Traveler

At the age of 26, Pallan left India. He had grown up there and never had been out of the country before. It was time for him to pursue his destiny in America. He worked as a tour guide for Indian Discovery USA. He took hordes of Indians on a bus across America, guiding them and providing accomodations and care.

Ten years later in 1992, as Pallan was the head of a travel company in North America. Carol Chaky worked for Travel and Leisure Magazine as a marketing assistant. She met Pallan Katgara at the Indian government tourist office at Rockerfeller Center.
